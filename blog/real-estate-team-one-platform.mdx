Every morning at 9 o'clock, Emily always opens four windows first: one is the company's housing resource backend, one is the client Excel, one is WhatsApp, and the other is the team shared calendar. She is neither a programmer nor a Product Manager, but a sales manager at a real estate company. The first thing she does after arriving at work every day is not to analyze the market or formulate sales plans, but to confirm whether there were any missed clients yesterday, which house viewing appointments there are today, and which housing resources need to be reallocated.

As the team size grows, this has almost become the most time-consuming task for her every day. The real problem has never been too few customers, but rather that more and more time is being occupied by repetitive work. Not only Emily is facing this problem, but every member of the team is deeply troubled by repetitive work and low efficiency, and all hope to solve these problems.

---

## 1. Problem: The number of customers is increasing, but work efficiency has not improved

The team receives hundreds of new inquiries every day, with customers coming from the official website, Google, Facebook, advertising campaigns, and friend referrals. Information keeps pouring in, but the real headache is that all this information is scattered in different places. As soon as a customer submits an inquiry, the salesperson needs to manually enter it into the CRM, and when scheduling a property viewing, it needs to be synchronized to the calendar.

If a client changes the time temporarily, the intermediary needs to be notified. After the client closes a deal, the Excel spreadsheet also needs to be updated. Each time a step is completed, the information needs to be re-entered.

No one finds these tasks difficult; it's just that they are repeated every day, and over time, the actual time spent communicating with clients has instead become less and less.

## 2. Instead of redesigning the business, they redesigned the tools

### a. Start from the business process, not from the page

Many software projects first design the pages and then consider the functions. This time, however, the team first asked a few questions.

What steps do homebuyers really need to complete? What is the most time-consuming task for real estate agents every day? What data does a sales manager most need to see every day?

After these questions have been answered, the framework of the entire product has gradually become clear.

Instead of creating a prototype first, they first sorted out the complete business process, which ultimately became the design blueprint for the entire product.

The team never thought of developing software just to create an app. What truly changed was that all work began to be completed on the same platform. They organized their business processes and, with the help of [AutoCoder](https://www.autocoder.cc/platform?utm_source=blog&utm_medium=latest&utm_campaign=RealEstateOnePlatform "AI app builder for full-stack business platforms"), quickly generated a real estate transaction platform covering Web, Mobile App, and Admin Dashboard to meet these requirements.

### b. Launch of multi-role trading platform

#### Buyer: Put the home purchase process in the same app

The new Buyer app covers the entire experience of homebuyers from searching for a home to closing a deal.

When opening the home page, users can see AI recommended listings, popular listings, and nearby properties for sale.

All housing listings are equipped with high-definition pictures, floor plans, VR house viewing, map locations, as well as information on surrounding schools, transportation, and living facilities.

Users can quickly filter by conditions such as city, price, housing type, area, school district, etc., or directly search for housing through the map.

After finding your ideal property, you can:

- Collect Property Listings
- Online Appointment for House Viewing
- Chat with the intermediary in real time
- Submit housing purchase requirements
- Get AI Property Recommendations
- Use the AI Mortgage Calculator
- Submit a quote online
- View the entire home purchase progress

Tasks that previously required switching between multiple platforms to complete are now all centralized in a single app.

In addition, a new AI feature has been added: AI will recommend more suitable housing options based on budget, location, and home-buying preferences, reducing the time users spend on repeated searches.

#### Agent: Let the intermediary allocate more time to customers

For intermediaries, the biggest changes occur in the backend.

After a new customer submits an inquiry, they will automatically enter the Lead list.

The system will automatically recommend suitable housing sources based on budget, location, and housing type requirements.

Appointment time is automatically synced to the calendar.

Chat history, customer remarks, follow-up status, and deal stage always remain consistent.

Agents no longer need to switch back and forth between CRM, chat software, and Excel.

Every day when you open the backend, you can directly see:

- Which customers were newly added today?
- Which customers need to be followed up?
- What are the viewing arrangements for today?
- Which customers have entered the negotiation stage?
- Which transactions are at risk?

As the time spent on organizing information decreases, the time spent on serving customers naturally increases.

## 3. AI has not replaced their work

The team initially thought that the biggest role of AI would be answering customer questions.

After actual use, they found that this was not the case. AI more often takes on the information that originally needed to be manually sorted.

For example:

- Automatically recommend housing units based on customer needs
- Automatically analyze Lead quality
- Intelligent Matching of Housing Sources
- Predicting housing price trends
- Automatically summarize customer communication records
- Calculate Loan Plan
- Remind customers who need follow-up
- Predict sales trends

AI does not make decisions for sales.

It simply completes those repetitive, mechanical, and time-consuming tasks in advance, enabling the team to focus more energy on what truly matters — serving customers.

## 4. Changes come from many small details

A few weeks after the platform went live, the team counted some changes in daily work:

| Job Content | Before | Now |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer Information Search | Multiple systems need to be opened | Viewable on a single page |
| Arrange a house viewing | Multiple phone conversations | Online reservation suffices to complete |
| Housing Recommendation | Manual screening for each set | AI Automatic Recommendation |
| View Team Performance | Organize Excel weekly | Real-time Dashboard |
| Customer Follow-up | Prone to omission | System Automatic Reminder |
| Sales Process | Scattered Records | Unified management of the entire process |
| Customer First Response Time | Approximately 4.8 hours | Approximately 22 minutes |
| Lead Follow-up Completion Rate | 61% | 91% |

These changes may not seem remarkable, but saving ten or dozens of minutes every day, when accumulated, allows the team to have more time to serve customers rather than deal with processes.

The team first sorted out three questions: What steps do homebuyers need to go through? What tasks do real estate agents actually need to complete each day? What data does the sales manager care about most?
