I'm not a technical clothes designer. Every time I heard words like *dashboard*, *backend*, or *tracking*, I'd assume it wasn't for me. But I kept running into the same problem.

I design and sell my own clothes through Instagram.

<Frame>
  ![Lune Atelier Instagram profile showing handmade jewelry and accessories posts](/images/blog/instagram-boutique-no-code-storefront/01.png)
</Frame>

Some posts do well. People DM me. Then it starts:

"Do you have this in S?"  
"How long does shipping take?"  
"Is this still available?"

I answer. Then I answered again. Then the same question tomorrow. What bothered me wasn't just the repetition — it was that I had **no visibility**. I didn't know:

- how many people actually wanted a piece
- which designs converted
- where people lost interest

Everything lived in conversations. No system. No structure. Just memory.

One moment made it obvious. Three people messaged me about the same jacket.

- one ready to buy
- one asking about sizing
- one still deciding

By the time I replied to all of them, it was sold out. I had to explain that three times. That's when it clicked: the problem wasn't demand; it was that everything lived in DMs.

I wrote down what I actually needed:

- a place to show pieces clearly
- a way for people to choose sizes and buy
- something that removes repetitive questions
- some visibility into what's happening

I then came across [autocoder.cc](https://www.autocoder.cc/platform?utm_source=blog&utm_medium=latest&utm_campaign=InstagramBoutiqueNoCode).
