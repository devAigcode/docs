## 1. The Power of the "First Sentence"

Yes, AutoCoder.cc can build a multi-role platform (e.g., an app for both Drivers and Passengers) from a simple prompt. Currently, the system excels at **linear logic chains** (up to 7 sequential steps).

**The Pro Tip:** Don't just stop at a vague idea. While you can say "Build a restaurant booking app," you get much better results by being a bit more descriptive.

- **Vague:** "I need a table reservation feature."
- **Architectural:** "I need a flow that includes a booking page, a visual seat layout with real-time vacancy status, a state-change trigger once a seat is occupied, and a final confirmation screen."

AutoCoder.cc provides a **Requirement List** before it ever writes a line of code. Think of this as your "Contract with the AI." You can edit, supplement, and refine this list on a visible page to ensure the logic is airtight before the "Factory" starts running.

---

## 2. UI vs. Logic: Know When to Take the Wheel

Not every change requires a "re-generation." Understanding the difference between **Visual Editing** and **Logic Modification** will save you hours.

- **For Simple Visuals:** If you just want to move a button, change a color, or tweak a text label, use the **Manual Editor**. It's faster, safer, and keeps the underlying logic intact.
- **For Complex Business Flows:** If you are changing *how* a feature works (e.g., adding an approval step to a payment), verify the entire flow first.
- **The Safety Net:** Complex logic changes currently have a higher "risk of failure." Always use the **Version Management** tool. Think of it as a "Time Machine." Before you start a risky logic overhaul, take note of your current version. If the AI "hallucinates" the fix, simply click to roll back.

## 3. The "Hidden" Powerhouse: The Admin Backend

Most users focus on the shiny app, but the real engine is the **Auto-Generated Backend**. AutoCoder.cc builds a management suite that covers 90% of business needs out-of-the-box. You can handle:

- **CRUD Operations:** Create, Read, Update, and Delete functions for Users, Products, or Content.
- **Data Analytics:** Built-in statistical reports and data visualization.
- **Custom Views:** You can define specific "Data Aggregation Pages" tailored to your unique business KPIs.

---

## 4. Winning the Mobile Game: "Flow-Based" Instructions

The mobile app is often where competitors (like Lovable or Base44) struggle to deliver "production-ready" beauty. AutoCoder.cc shines here, but you need to feed it **sequential logic**.

To get a professional-grade app, describe the **User Journey** step-by-step.

> **Example Flow:** "The Teacher creates a quiz → The Student submits answers → The Teacher grades the quiz → The Student views the final result."

By clearly defining the **Previous Page → The Action → The Next Page**, you give the AI the architectural "map" it needs to ensure the UI flows naturally and the data follows the user.

## Case Study: The "Gold Standard" Blueprint

To show these engineering tips in action, let's design a **Multi-Role Learning Management System (LMS)**. This scenario involves Teachers and Students, requiring a synchronized flow across the Frontend, Mobile App, and a robust Backend.

Here is the "Gold Standard" approach to prompting and managing this project in AutoCoder.cc.

---

### 1. The Initial "Vibe" Prompt

Instead of just saying "make a school app," we use a **Flow-Based Instruction** that defines the roles and the linear logic chain immediately.

**Prompt:**

```
Build a full-stack LMS called 'EduFlow' with two roles: Teacher and Student. The core flow is:
1. Teacher creates a quiz with 5 questions.
2. Student receives a notification and views the quiz on the mobile app.
3. Student submits answers.
4. Teacher views submissions in the Admin Backend and enters a score.
5. Student receives their grade and a 'Result Summary' page.
6. I need a professional mobile UI for students and a data-heavy dashboard for teachers.
```

---

### 2. The "Requirement List" Phase (The First Checkpoint)

Once you hit enter, AutoCoder.cc generates a **Requirement List**. Before the AI starts writing code, this is where you apply Engineering Tip #1: **Supplement the Logic.**

What you should add/edit on that page:

- **Edge Case Logic:** "If a student exits the app mid-quiz, save their progress as a draft."
- **Visual Specifics:** "On the 'Result Summary' page, show a circular progress bar for the score and a list of incorrect answers."
- **Validation:** "The Teacher cannot submit a score higher than 100."

---

### 3. Managing the Build: Logic vs. Manual Tweaks

As the system builds the Node-Tree, you will see the pages appear. This is where you apply Tip #2 & #3.

- **The Manual Tweak:** You notice the "Submit" button is too small or the wrong shade of blue. Don't ask the AI to fix it. Use the Manual Editor to change the CSS or padding. This keeps the AI focused on the heavy lifting.
- **The Logic Overhaul:** You decide you want a 3rd role—an Admin who can delete users. This is a "Risk Operation."
  - **Step A:** Note your current version number in the Version Management tab.
  - **Step B:** Tell the AI: "Add an Admin role that can access the 'User Management' table in the backend."
  - **Step C:** If the AI breaks the existing Teacher flow, simply Rollback to your saved version and try a more incremental prompt.

---

### 4. Leveraging the "Industrial" Backend

While the Student is using the beautiful Mobile App, the Teacher needs to manage 100+ students.

**How to optimize the Backend:**

- **Custom Aggregation:** Use the backend editor to create a "High-Risk Student" page. Tell the AI: "Create a custom dashboard view that aggregates all students with an average score below 60%."
- **Data Export:** Since AutoCoder.cc generates native logic, you can easily add a button to "Export all grades to CSV"—a feature most "Vibe" tools struggle to implement without external plugins.
